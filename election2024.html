Counties to watch carefully

  1. Some bellwhether counties (which may not matter much for state totals but tend to correlate with the winners) include: Erie, Northampton, Chester in PA; Door and Ozaukee in WI.
  2. Allegheny County, PA, tends to finish counting early. In 2020, Biden won with 59%. In 2022, Fetterman won with 63%.
  3. Waukesha, Ozaukee, and Washington in WI are all Republican counties that showed Democratic gain in 2020 compared to 2016, and are Milwaukee suburbs that may signal any shifts in suburban voters.

The Luke-o-Meter Election Prediction Tool

A non-scientific, highly inaccurate model based on the vibes of a random 31 year old.

Who do I want to win? Harris (because I have object permanence and above room-temp IQ).

What are the odds of each candidate as of Oct. 25, 2024?

Trump: 90%

Harris: 10%

Why does the Luke-o-Meter think this when other, far more sophisticated models say it's approximately 50/50?

  1. Consistently weak polling for Harris across October, more than just noise.
  2. People correctly label the Trump campaign as chaotic and bizarre, but incorrectly conclude that it's poorly run. People are talking about him, people are thinking about him, and that's all that matters with low-information voters who have never thought about policy in their entire lives (many of these voters would vote for Harris if she played her hand right).
  3. Harris campaign failures: they stopped doing all the things from the summer that had people talking about them. Hammering Republicans as "weird" was brilliant and spoke to apolitical people. Instead they dropped that and decided to lean into the Cheneys. Few remember the Bush/Cheney war hawk days fondly (or at least they claim not to despite being war hawks themselves), and the kind of person who even knows who Liz Cheney is is already hyper-partisan one way or the other.

I assign a baseline 10% chance to either candidate to account for a 40-year upset type of event or polling miss, hence why Harris's chances will not go below 10%. The Luke-o-Meter will not update during Election Day, even if incoming ballot counts swing it massively in either direction.

Oct. 29, 2024 update: no change to the odds. A few favorable polls for Harris were canceled out by many more favorable polls for Trump. Early voting, while unreliable as an indicator, is not providing any good signs for Democrats, and are at best neutral; most indicate strong GOP turnout. Another strategic mistep by the Harris campaign was, weirdly enough, to not have either Harris or Walz appear on the Joe Rogan podcast. JR's meathead, MAGA-curious audience are, I feel, nonetheless partly reachable by the Harris campaign at the margins. Though Harris's reasons for declining the interview are understandable given the time-sink this close to election day (3 hours interview + hours of travel to and from Austin, TX), she nevertheless could benefit greatly from appearing on it given the immense reach of the show. That said, JR's refusal to be flown out by the campaign of a presidential candidate and sitting Vice President indicates that his view of himself is sufficiently inflated as to indicate he might not have been a soft interviewer. With Vance now scheduled to appear on JR's podcast, Trump's chances of winning would be improved if they were not already at the maximum the Luke-o-Meter assigns him. If Harris loses, her and Walz's lack of appearance on this show and other similar alternative media outlets may be viewed as the same kind of misstep that Sec. Clinton made in 2016 by not adequately visiting Upper Midwest states. The only strategic misstep of the Trump campaign this week was having a racist comedian at his NYC rally, saying something similarly terrible as many other statements made by Trump and associates. It might help Harris in swing states, especially Pennsylvania, but it is likely to be marginal and largely forgotten by Election Day.

Oct. 31, 2024 update: no change in the model. Upon examining week-old information on Pennsylvania voter registration changes, the Luke-o-Meter state predictor (proprietary, not generally publically available) projects Trump winning PA by 248k votes, or a 3.6% margin, which is in the range of recent polls. Because Biden won the state in 2020 by 80k, and Trump won it in 2016 by 44k, this figure of Trump +248k is likely an overestimation, and if independents break more heavily for Harris than they did for Biden, she could reduce the margin, though it likely wouldn't be enough without a substantial enthusiasm gap in her favor. To account for this possibility, the model thinks that R+248k is a soft ceiling for Trump, and he will likely win the state by a smaller margin. The national Luke-o-Meter is built heavily on swing state trends (which are positive for Trump) and lightly on national (positive for Trump) and other state trends (apparently neutral for either candidate, as most non-swing states are polling very similarly to 2020). If PA registration numbers were positive for Democrats, it would have heavily swung the model in their favor, but given anemic performance of Democratic registration following a good performance in 2022, the national model is predicting a 90% chance of a Trump win. Similar shifts toward Republican registration growth in Florida in the lead up to the 2020 election suggested a Trump win, which is what happened, and by a larger margin than 2020. In much the same way that Democrats appeared to throw up their hands and give up on Florida and Ohio (states that Obama won twice), the party's cowardly complacency and accomodations toward rising fascism means that, if PA follows this model's prediction, Democrats will likely give up on it as well, instead of aggressively pushing back like a competent party should.
